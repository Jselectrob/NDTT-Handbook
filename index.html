About This Game

NDTT (Non-Descript Table Top RPG) is a community-driven fantasy role-playing game that empowers players to create their own custom classes, races, backstories, items, weapons, campaigns, and abilities. The core philosophy is creative freedom balanced with fair gameplay.

What Makes This Different?

NDTT features a unique Ability Point system that allows players to literally build their own abilities from scratch. Every character is truly unique, shaped by the player's imagination and strategic choices. The game grows with your character across multiple campaigns, creating a persistent hero that ages and evolves with you.

Character Creation Overview

Characters are built using six core attributes and a pool of Ability Points that you spend to create your own custom abilities. Higher-cost abilities are more powerful but harder to successfully execute, creating natural balance through risk vs. reward.

Campaign Persistence

Core Attributes

Every character has six core attributes that define their capabilities:

Strength (STR) - Physical power and combat prowess. Adds directly to physical damage dealt.

Defense (DEF) - Overall defensive capability, encompassing dodge, armor, and damage reduction.

Vitality (VIT) - Health points (HP). Determines how much damage you can take before falling.

Intelligence (INT) - Mental acuity and magical power. Adds directly to magic-based damage.

Wisdom (WIS) - Perception, insight, and magical understanding. Adds directly to magic-based damage.

Charisma (CHA) - Force of personality, persuasiveness, and leadership ability.

Ability Point System

The heart of NDTT's character customization is the Ability Point system. Players use these points to create their own unique abilities.

Ability Point Progression

Starting Points: 10 ability points at character creation

Level Gain: +2 ability points per level

Maximum Cap: 30 ability points total

Creating Abilities

When you create an ability, you spend ability points from your pool. The more points you invest, the more powerful the ability becomes.

Important Balance Mechanic: Higher-cost abilities are more powerful but HARDER to successfully execute. They require higher dice rolls to hit/succeed, creating a natural risk vs. reward system.

Example: If you spend all 10 starting points on one devastating ability, it will be incredibly powerful but very difficult to land without a high roll. Alternatively, you could create five 2-point abilities that are weaker but more reliable.

Using Abilities in Combat

When you use an ability, it costs resources (typically HP, Defense, or other stats). The player chooses what resource is consumed based on the nature of the ability - a blood magic spell might cost HP, while a defensive technique might temporarily reduce Defense.

Dice Resolution: NDTT uses a d20 for ability checks and combat resolution.

Ability Modifications

During Campaign: Characters are locked - you cannot respec existing abilities, but you CAN create new abilities as you gain ability points from leveling.

Between Campaigns: Before starting a new campaign, you can respec your abilities, changing their power and cost to better fit your evolving strategy.

Combat System

NDTT uses a traditional turn-based combat system with d20 resolution.

Character Advancement

Characters level up through experience, gaining +2 ability points per level. Your character persists across campaigns, aging and growing more powerful with each adventure.

Mechanics In Development

Classes in NDTT

Unlike traditional RPGs, NDTT doesn't strictly define classes. Instead, classes emerge organically from your ability choices, attribute distribution, and playstyle. However, we provide archetypal class concepts as inspiration and starting points.

Martial Archetypes

Warrior - Focus on STR and DEF, create abilities centered on physical combat and weapon mastery.

Paladin - Balance STR with WIS/INT, combine physical abilities with supportive or divine-themed powers.

Ranger - Blend DEF and tactical abilities focused on precision and battlefield control.

Magical Archetypes

Wizard - Maximize INT, invest in high-cost devastating spells that require careful timing.

Sorcerer - Balance INT with multiple mid-cost abilities for versatility.

Warlock - Create unique pact-themed abilities with interesting resource costs.

Druid - Mix WIS-based abilities with nature-themed transformations or summons.

Support Archetypes

Cleric - Focus on WIS, create healing and protective abilities.

Healer - Specialize entirely in restorative abilities with low costs for consistency.

Bard - Use CHA, create abilities that buff allies or debuff enemies.

Dancer - Blend DEF and CHA for movement-based support abilities.

Specialist Archetypes

Rogue - Focus on DEF and STR, create precise strike abilities with tactical advantages.

Alchemist - Mix INT with utility abilities like buffs, debuffs, and environmental effects.

Custom Class Guidelines

The beauty of NDTT is that these are just suggestions. Your "class" is defined by:

‚Ä¢ Your attribute distribution

‚Ä¢ The abilities you create

‚Ä¢ Your playstyle and strategy

‚Ä¢ Your character's story and background

Community Contributions: Players are encouraged to share their unique class concepts, ability combinations, and creative builds. As long as abilities follow the core balance mechanics (higher cost = harder to hit), anything goes!

Balance Philosophy: The ability point system naturally prevents overpowered builds. If you invest everything in one massive ability, you're vulnerable. If you spread too thin, you lack impact. Finding the sweet spot is part of the game.
